About the program

Built for practitioners who want to lead in clinical research

Whether you're early in your career or pivoting deeper into trials methodology, this program meets you where you are and takes you further.

Cohort learning model

You'll move through the program alongside a carefully selected group of 20 peers, building professional relationships that extend beyond the certificate itself.

Applied from the start

Every module pairs recorded instruction with a live interactive session. You're not just absorbing content — you're working with it alongside faculty and peers.

Culminates in a capstone

The program closes with a practicum and capstone project presented to the cohort and program faculty — a tangible demonstration of your expertise.

ASA-credentialed

Earn a certificate from the American Statistical Association.

Program model

The eighteen modules, a practicum, and a capstone are designed as a complete arc from foundation to applied expertise.

Part 1 of each module

90-minute recorded session

A pre-recorded instructional video available on demand. Watch on your own schedule, pause, and revisit as needed. Recorded sessions remain accessible throughout the entire program including during scheduled breaks.

Some modules include embedded assessments
Part 2 of each module

90-minute live interactive session

A synchronous session with faculty and the full cohort. Facilitated discussion, case application, and Q&A build on the recorded material and connect it to real-world practice.

Some sessions include live assessments
Pacing & schedule

Designed to fit professional life

Sessions run on a weekly cadence with planned breaks giving you time to absorb material, manage your workload, and show up ready.

Modules 1–6

Foundations

Foundations of clinical research: trial design across early, middle, and late phases; writing objectives; selecting endpoints; and core principles of randomization.
Modules 7–12

Intermediate methods

Statistical methodology in depth: power and sample size, analysis populations and methods, analysis principles, interim analyses and monitoring, adaptive and Bayesian designs, and biomarkers in precision medicine.
Modules 13–18

Advanced topics

Advanced and applied topics: specialized trial designs, dissemination and reporting, protocol development and execution, regulation and oversight, data capture and management, and emerging trends shaping the future of clinical trials.
Final phase

Practicum + Capstone

Applied project with faculty guidance, closing with a capstone presentation to the cohort.
Flexible access: Recorded sessions are available throughout the program.
Who should apply

This program is for you if…

The cohort is designed to bring together a range of backgrounds and career stages. We're looking for people ready to engage seriously with the material and contribute to each other's learning.

Early career

Statisticians & biostatisticians building a foundation

You have a degree in statistics, biostatistics, or a quantitative field and want structured, expert-led preparation for clinical trials work.

Mid-career

Professionals deepening or redirecting their expertise

You work in industry, biotech, academia, or government and want to formalize your knowledge or transition more fully into trials methodology.

Academic & research

Researchers engaged in clinical study design

You collaborate on or lead clinical research and want deeper statistical grounding to strengthen your contributions to study teams.

Data science

Data scientists moving into regulated research

You bring strong quantitative skills and want to understand the specific frameworks, standards, and methods that govern clinical trials.
